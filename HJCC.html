About the committee

"It was not well to drive men into final corners; at those moments they could all develop teeth and claws."
- Stephen Crane
From Fort Sumter to Palmito Ranch, the four years that comprised the early 1860s have been responsible for seeing one of history's greatest injustices overturned. The price? Nearly tearing a young nation into two.
Seldom are we given the chance to rewrite an entire history. Death by dysentery, amputation, and surgery claim the lives of generations of Americans. In American households, human beings find themselves stripped of their freedom, born into lives where they are refused their independence for the colour of their skin. As representatives of either the Union or the Confederacy, choose your gambits with caution. The rifles of revolution are nearby; deliver your justice carefully.
