About the committee

How much power does the UN have? The state of Israel probably has the best answer.
It is the 14th of May, 1948. On the eastern edge of the Mediterranean, David Ben-Gurion has just declared the independence of the State of Israel on what was formerly the British Mandate of Palestine. After centuries of oppression, the Levites have finally returned to the Holy Land.
However, their new State has not only uprooted the lives of the former Palestinians, but has also taken two of the holiest sites of the Arab world from them. The Arabs cannot tolerate this, and within the next few decades, the Holy Land will descend into a war intertwined with religion, race, and culture.
Delegates, history is a fickle thing, written only by those powerful enough to dictate it. Wield the power to create a new country as your decisions will potentially change the face of the Middle East for decades to come. Welcome to the Historic United Nations Special Committee On Palestine.
